Educational background

My journey in Physics and Computer Science

My search for knowledge quickly led me to University, where I began my path studying one of the oldest sciences: physics. Only after did I fall in love, ironically, with one the youngest sciences: I decided I would work in technology, and added CS to my education.

Combining natural science with technology

When choosing to learn physics, I knew this would imply doing lots of math and solving difficult problems. It did not occur to me, perhaps foolishly so, that with our current state of technological advancement, knowledge of computer programming is just as important as the understanding of physical processes. After taking a computational physics course in my first semester, I instantly adored the sheer fun that computer science provided me, and saw the immense potential of technology for the advancement of the discipline. Now, my background includes both a solid basis in many areas of physics, expertise in mathematics (calculus, linear algebra, complex analysis and more) and most importantly, the ability to apply them using technological tools (Python, Mathematica etc).

Truth is, I'm a guy that simply enjoys learning complicated things and using them to solve problems. My love is not limited to physics, math, or computers; I simply like technicality in all it's forms.
